## Entropy Weighting

The empirical weighting structure is highly concentrated in a few indicators within each pillar.

Economic:

- Total subsidies/Farm Family Income: `0.365`
- Labour Productivity: `0.329`
- Capital Productivity: `0.196`

Environmental:

- Water Intensity: `0.395`
- Pesticide usage: `0.176`
- Livestock density: `0.155`

Social:

- Structural change rate: `0.375`
- Other gainful activities/Total output: `0.262`
- Family Farm Income/Reference Income: `0.157`

These values confirm that cross-country differentiation is driven less by the average level of every indicator and more by the indicators that vary most strongly across Member States.

## Score Profiles

Economic leaders in the current run are Italy and Denmark, followed by Portugal, Romania, and the Netherlands. The lowest economic scores are observed in Slovakia, Czechia, Finland, Slovenia, and Estonia.

Environmental performance is led by Austria, Sweden, Luxembourg, Estonia, and Finland. The weakest environmental scores are Malta, Cyprus, Bulgaria, Spain, and Greece.

Social performance is led by Slovakia, Hungary, and Slovenia, followed by Estonia and Sweden. The lowest social scores are Romania, Bulgaria, Malta, Finland, and Spain.

## Cluster Structure

The gap-statistic one-standard-error rule selects `k = 2`. This choice is important to explain clearly in the report: although higher values of `k` can still produce larger raw gap values, the one-standard-error rule deliberately chooses the smallest parsimonious solution that is not meaningfully outperformed by the next candidate.

The two clusters are:

- Cluster 1 (`n = 7`): `BG, CY, EL, ES, IT, MT, PT`
- Cluster 2 (`n = 20`): `AT, BE, CZ, DE, DK, EE, FI, FR, HR, HU, IE, LT, LU, LV, NL, PL, RO, SE, SI, SK`

Average cluster profiles:

- Cluster 1: Economic `0.585`, Environmental `0.450`, Social `0.398`
- Cluster 2: Economic `0.433`, Environmental `0.823`, Social `0.524`

This supports the interpretation of Cluster 1 as an economically stronger but environmentally constrained regime, while Cluster 2 represents a broader environmental-social advantage regime with more moderate economic performance on average.

## Reporting Guidance

The report should emphasize three points:

1. The dimensions do not move in lockstep; cross-country trade-offs remain central.
2. The cluster split is not a simplistic geographic North-South divide, even if Mediterranean countries are visibly concentrated in Cluster 1.
3. The results are comparative and exploratory, not causal.
